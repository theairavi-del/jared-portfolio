01

Gardens

Nolita, New York City

Professor Montoya, D6, 2025

A community-first proposal developed from Manhattan borough research. The project expands green infrastructure and reframes urban housing through light, planting systems, and public space.

The Connection and The Gateway test expression, social exchange, and environmental growth as one architectural system.

In collaboration with Breck Crane

02

Rosion

Charleston, South Carolina

Professor Maze, D5, 2024

Rosion is a music school shaped by Charleston's rivers and shifting edge conditions. A central path acts as the project's organizing force, guiding users from street to courtyard.

Program includes concert hall, workshop, practice spaces, reading rooms, and mixed-use collaboration zones.

03

WerkPlatz

Gainesville, Florida

Professor Maze, D5, 2024

WerkPlatz frames making as civic culture. The ground level curates public exhibition while the upper level supports fabrication, archive, and learning.

The design choreographs transitions between high-energy workshop zones and restorative unconditioned thresholds.

04

Automaton

Amelia Island, Florida

Professor Cronin, D7, 2025

Physical research into textile architecture through iterative prototyping and full-scale deployment. The structure behaves like a responsive body in wind-heavy coastal conditions.

PVC armature and light membrane system were tested through mapping, build sequencing, and field documentation.

In collaboration with Ahmik Paul, Chris Muratti, and Jackson Rowden

05

Meditation

My Sketchbook

Jared Boardman, Ongoing

Drawing and watercolor are both thinking tools and discipline. These studies sharpen observation, speed ideation, and support architectural decision-making beyond studio deliverables.

The collection combines field sketches, personal studies, and atmospheric color work.
